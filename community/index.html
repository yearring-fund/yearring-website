Why we exist

Built responsibly.
Observed seriously.

YearRing Fund Protocol is being built as long-term financial infrastructure — a programmable vault layer that lets users deposit, lock, and structure commitments on-chain, with a beneficiary and governance layer on top.

Building that responsibly requires a small, serious group of early observers and testers who care about system design, not just returns. This community is where that group forms.

We are not looking for a large following. We are looking for the right people.

What the community does

Five things.
Each one matters.

Observe
Follow protocol development milestones, architectural decisions, and on-chain deployments as they happen — not through a marketing lens.
Discuss
Exchange questions and feedback on protocol design, risk structures, fee mechanics, and governance systems with people who actually read the docs.
Test
Invited members may participate in controlled-access testing rounds on Base Mainnet. Testing access is reviewed and invitation-based. It is not sold, traded, or distributed through airdrop-style campaigns.
Report
Surface edge cases, UI issues, documentation gaps, and security observations through the proper channels — not publicly, not speculatively.
Signal
Provide structured feedback that informs future protocol decisions. Not hype, not pressure — considered input from people who understand what they are commenting on.
Early tester path

How access works.

Testing access is controlled and invitation-based. No public mint, whitelist competition, or points campaign is active at this stage. Access is granted based on demonstrated interest and fit.

01
Join the community
Follow the official channels listed below and read the available documentation. Understand the protocol before requesting access.
02
Engage substantively
Ask or answer questions that show system understanding. Demonstrate that you read docs and think carefully — not that you are enthusiastic.
03
Express interest
If a testing round is open, express interest through the designated process. Do not message team members directly to ask for access.
04
Receive an official invitation
If access is granted, you will be contacted through verifiable official channels — never through a private message requesting any wallet action or personal key.
05
Test and report
Use the protocol as directed. Document what you observe. Submit structured feedback. Your keys remain yours — we will never ask for them under any circumstance.
Join Discord to express interest →

No access is guaranteed. Expressions of interest are reviewed manually and responded to through official channels only.

Community rules

Seven rules.
Non-negotiable.

01
Discuss the protocol, not speculative pricing. No public token, airdrop, or points campaign is active at this stage. Speculation, fake allocation claims, and price-focused discussion are off-topic.
02
Be precise. Vague questions get vague answers. Show your reasoning.
03
No promotion of other protocols, tokens, or services in community spaces.
04
No unsolicited financial advice, yield comparisons, or return projections.
05
Respect the early-stage nature of this project. Unrealistic expectations create pressure that harms careful development.
06
Use the designated feedback and bug-report channels. Do not post sensitive findings publicly before responsible disclosure.
07
Team members communicate through official, verifiable accounts only. Report impersonators immediately — do not engage with them.
—
Violations may result in removal. Serious violations — impersonation, phishing, coordinated manipulation — will be escalated.
